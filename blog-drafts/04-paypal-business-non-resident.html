How to Open a PayPal Business Account as a Non-Resident in 2025

PayPal Business is one of the most requested payment solutions for MEA founders selling internationally. But PayPal's direct signup from Morocco, Algeria, or Tunisia comes with major limitations — restricted withdrawals, limited features, and frequent account freezes. The solution that actually works: a US LLC with a properly set up PayPal Business account. Here is how to do it.

PayPal in Morocco: The Problem

Moroccan PayPal accounts are highly restricted. While you can technically create a PayPal account with a Moroccan address, you will quickly run into serious limitations:

  • You cannot withdraw funds to a Moroccan bank account directly
  • Your account is regularly flagged for review when receiving international payments
  • You cannot access PayPal's full business features (invoicing, multi-currency, subscriptions)
  • Account freezes are common for sellers receiving volume payments

The root cause is the same as Stripe: Morocco's foreign exchange regulations create friction for international payment processors. A US LLC solves every single one of these problems.

PayPal Business with a US LLC: How It Works

When you form a US LLC, you become the owner of a legal US business. You can open a PayPal Business account in the name of your LLC — using your US address, EIN, and US phone number. This gives you a fully functional US PayPal Business account with:

  • No withdrawal restrictions
  • Full access to PayPal's business features
  • USD balance management
  • The ability to send and receive from any country PayPal supports
  • Integration with Shopify, WooCommerce, and most major platforms

What You Need to Open PayPal Business via US LLC

  • US LLC formation documents (Articles of Organization)
  • EIN (US Employer Identification Number)
  • US address (registered agent address)
  • US phone number
  • US bank account (for linking to PayPal)
  • Your passport (beneficial owner verification)

Step-by-Step: Opening PayPal Business as a Non-Resident

Step 1 — Form your US LLC

Your LLC needs to be fully formed and have a valid EIN before you apply for PayPal. PayPal Business requires EIN verification during the account setup process.

Step 2 — Get a US phone number

PayPal will send verification codes to your phone number. You need a reliable US number that can receive SMS. LaunchBridge provides a dedicated US phone number as part of every package.

Step 3 — Link a US bank account

PayPal Business requires a linked US bank account. Mercury Bank (free, fully online, non-resident friendly) is the most popular choice. Once linked, PayPal can pay out to Mercury, and you can transfer from Mercury to your Wise account for conversion to MAD.

Step 4 — Set up your PayPal Business account

Create the account using your LLC name, EIN, US address, and US phone number. Complete the business verification process by providing your LLC documents. LaunchBridge handles all of this as part of the Ultimate package.

Step 5 — Connect to your store

Once verified, connect PayPal to your Shopify store, website, or wherever you sell. You can accept credit cards and PayPal balance payments from customers worldwide.

How Do I Transfer Money from PayPal to Morocco?

The recommended flow is:

  1. PayPal Business (USD) → Mercury Bank (USD)
  2. Mercury Bank → Wise Business (USD → MAD transfer)
  3. Wise → Moroccan bank account

Wise Business supports USD→MAD transfers with real exchange rates and provides full documentation for Office des Changes compliance. Transfer fees are typically 0.5–1% — far cheaper than traditional bank wires.

Will PayPal Freeze My Account?

Account freezes are common on PayPal globally — not specifically for non-residents. The best way to avoid freezes is to:

  • Complete full business verification upfront (LLC docs + EIN)
  • Maintain consistent transaction patterns
  • Have a proper business website with clear product/service descriptions
  • Respond quickly to any PayPal information requests

A properly set-up US LLC account is far less likely to be flagged than an unverified or personal account.
