How to Get Stripe in Morocco Legally in 2025 (Via US LLC)

Stripe does not support Morocco as a merchant country. If you try to sign up for Stripe with a Moroccan address, you will hit a wall. But thousands of Moroccan founders are using Stripe legally every day — through a US LLC. Here is exactly how it works.

Why Stripe Is Not Available Directly in Morocco

Stripe's availability depends on local banking regulations, currency controls, and financial infrastructure. Morocco has strict foreign exchange controls governed by the Office des Changes, which makes it difficult for international payment processors to operate there under local regulation.

This is not a permanent situation — Stripe has been expanding to new countries each year. But in 2025, Morocco is still not on Stripe's supported countries list. Neither are Algeria, Tunisia, or most other African countries outside of South Africa and Nigeria (which have limited access).

The Legal Solution: A US LLC

A US LLC (Limited Liability Company) is a legal US business entity. When you form a US LLC, you can open a Stripe account as that US business — even though you personally live in Morocco. You are not pretending to be American. You are operating a legally registered US company of which you are the foreign owner.

This is completely legal under both US law and Moroccan law. The IRS has specific provisions for non-resident LLC owners. Stripe's terms of service allow non-US residents to operate US business accounts.

What You Need to Open Stripe with a US LLC

To open a Stripe account for your US LLC, you need:

  • A registered US LLC — Articles of Organization from your chosen state
  • An EIN — your US tax identification number (like a company SSN)
  • A US address — provided by your registered agent
  • A US phone number — for Stripe verification and 2FA
  • A US bank account — to receive Stripe payouts (Mercury or Relay work well)
  • Your passport — for identity verification as the beneficial owner

Is This Legal Under Moroccan Law?

Yes. Moroccan law allows citizens to own foreign businesses. Receiving income from a foreign company you own is subject to normal Moroccan income tax declaration requirements. LaunchBridge recommends consulting a Moroccan accountant (fiduciaire) to ensure your tax declarations are in order.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using a personal US address — Using a friend's address instead of a proper registered agent can cause legal and tax problems.
  • Using VoIP numbers — Some free US phone number apps are flagged by Stripe. Use a proper US number.
  • Applying before the EIN is issued — Stripe will request your EIN during verification. Apply only after you have it.
  • Using a personal bank account for payouts — Always use a business account (Mercury, Relay) that matches your LLC name.
