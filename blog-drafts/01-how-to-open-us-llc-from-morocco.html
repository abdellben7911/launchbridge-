A US LLC solves this. As a non-resident owner of a US company, you can legally open a Stripe account, receive PayPal Business payments, and connect to the full stack of global fintech tools — all while operating from Morocco.

What Is a US LLC?

An LLC (Limited Liability Company) is the most popular business structure in the United States. It combines the legal protection of a corporation with the tax simplicity of a sole proprietorship. For non-residents, it offers a critical advantage: a single-member LLC owned by a non-US person is treated as a "disregarded entity" for US tax purposes — meaning no US corporate tax if you have no US-based income.

You pay taxes in Morocco on your worldwide income, as you normally would. The LLC simply gives you a legal US business identity.

Which US State Should You Choose?

Three states are best for non-residents: Wyoming, Montana, and New Mexico. Here is a quick comparison:

  • Wyoming — Most popular for non-residents. Strong privacy laws, no state income tax, and widely accepted by Stripe and PayPal. Best if you want maximum credibility.
  • Montana — Fastest and most affordable. No sales tax. Ideal for founders who want to start quickly at lower cost.
  • New Mexico — Cheapest option. No annual report required. Perfect for beginners testing the waters.

What Do You Need to Open a US LLC from Morocco?

The requirements are simpler than most people expect:

  • A valid passport (no US visa required)
  • A business name
  • A registered agent address in the chosen state (LaunchBridge provides this)
  • An EIN (Employer Identification Number) — your US tax ID

You do not need to travel to the US. You do not need a US bank account to start. Everything is done remotely.

Is It Legal?

Yes. Non-US residents are legally allowed to own and operate a US LLC. There is no requirement to be a US citizen or resident. Hundreds of thousands of foreign nationals own US LLCs. The IRS has specific procedures for non-resident LLC owners (Form 5472 and Form 1120 for reporting purposes, which LaunchBridge can guide you through).

Do I Need to Pay US Taxes?

A single-member LLC owned by a non-resident with no US-sourced income and no US employees is generally not subject to US federal income tax. You will however have annual filing obligations (Form 5472 + Form 1120). LaunchBridge can connect you with a US CPA to handle this.
